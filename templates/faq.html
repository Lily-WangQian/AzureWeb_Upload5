Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Sustainability Report Alignment (SRA) tool?

The Sustainability Report Alignment (SRA) tool is an AI-based analytical tool that compares financial institutions’ sustainability or ESG reports with established sustainability standards and regulations. Its purpose is to assess how closely a report aligns with a selected standard or regulation and to identify similarities, gaps, and differences in content.

Why is report alignment with sustainability standards important?

Sustainable finance reporting is often heterogeneous and inconsistent, frequently omitting key themes such as financed emissions or addressing them in non-standardized ways. As global standards and regulations—such as IFRS S1 and S2—gain importance, alignment with established frameworks becomes increasingly necessary for transparency, comparability, and credibility.

What problem does the SRA tool address?

The SRA tool addresses the gap between sustainability standards and actual industry reporting practices. It helps identify where financial institutions’ sustainability disclosures align with, or diverge from, regulatory and standard-setting expectations across environmental, social, and governance (ESG) dimensions.

How does the SRA tool work?

Users upload a sustainability or ESG report in PDF format and select a sustainability standard or regulation from a dropdown menu. The tool then analyzes the uploaded report and displays the results once the analysis is complete.

What types of standards and regulations are included?

The SRA database contains sustainability standards and regulatory documents identified through academic literature and systematic internet research. The current database includes 88 standards and regulatory documents, which are periodically updated.

What methods does the SRA tool use to compare documents?

The analysis relies on artificial intelligence models, including topic models (LDA), BERT-based language models, and generative AI approaches. BERT models are particularly well-suited because they capture contextual and semantic relationships between words in complex regulatory and reporting texts.

How are keywords identified in the analysis?

The tool extracts keywords using two approaches: TF-IDF (Term Frequency–Inverse Document Frequency) and contextual keyword extraction using a large language embedding model. These keyword sets are then combined to create a more comprehensive representation of each document’s content.

How is similarity between a report and a standard measured?

Similarity is calculated using cosine similarity, which compares documents as vectors in a high-dimensional space. A higher cosine similarity score indicates stronger alignment between the sustainability report and the selected standard or regulation.

What does the similarity score mean?

The similarity score ranges from –1 to 1. A score closer to 1 indicates a high degree of alignment between the report and the standard, while a score closer to 0 indicates little similarity.

Does the SRA tool evaluate report quality or compliance?

No. The SRA tool does not assess the quality, accuracy, or regulatory compliance of disclosures. It measures textual and thematic alignment between documents and standards.

Who is the SRA tool designed for?

The tool is designed for researchers, regulators, financial institutions, students, and policy analysts interested in sustainability reporting alignment.

Can the SRA tool be used for comparative analysis?

Yes. The tool supports comparisons across different reports and standards, enabling benchmarking and longitudinal analysis.

What are the limitations of the SRA tool?

As a text-based analytical tool, the SRA focuses on language and content rather than underlying data quality or verification. Results should be interpreted as indicative of alignment in reporting language.
